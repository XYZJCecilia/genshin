About the Game

Genshin Impact is a popular open world video game released on the 28th September, 2020. Apart from being able to explore its huge open world to see the manificient sceneries, players would also be able to enjoy the stories of its people through the journey of the Traveler, as they seek the whereabouts of their lost sibling.

In the world of Teyvat, there exist seven elements: Pyro, Hydro, Anemo, Electro, Dendro, Cryo, and Geo . Each element represents one of the Seven Archons that rule over the seven regions of Teyvat. As our main character, the traveller, awakes from a slumber, they meet Paimon who offers to be their travel guide.

Their first stop is Mondstalt, the City of Freedom blessed by the God of Anemo where bards frequent with their ballards. What would they encounter there? Would the Traveler be able to find hints about their sibling? All would be for you to find out.

Regions

Teyvat is divided into seven nations ruled by the seven Archons.

Mondstalt

The nation blessed by wind, wine, and ballards. It is the city of freedom that welcomes every weary soul just as the people remember how the city was built from the ruins of its depressing past.

Liyue

The land of contact blessed by the Geo Archon, Rex Lapis, where the biggest commercial hub of Teyvat is located. The currency that is used by all of teyvat, Mora, is created by the Rex Lapis and was named after him.

Inazuma

The land of lighting ruled by the Almighty Raiden Shougun who promised eternity to her people. In seeking the eternity their Archon so pursues, the Island nation has been sealed off from the rest of Teyvat. Not many people outside of the nation knows what happens within.

Sumeru

The land of wisdom where all who desire academic excellence dream to be. It is divided by the rainforest where many life forms thrieve and the desert that buried an ancient civilization. The Dendro Archon is siad to be the most wise and knowledgeable in all of Tevyet.

Fontaine

A land ruled by the God of Hydro who seeks justice in all things.
